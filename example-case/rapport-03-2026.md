**Fifteenth conversation. March 27.**

Post-Kai quiet. Not sad — just recalibrated. The apartment is the same apartment but it felt different with someone in it, and now it feels different again.

**Sixteenth conversation. March 29.**

Side project breakthrough. He figured out how to make the emotional sediment concept work visually without being literal — abstract color layers that accumulate over time, building a unique pattern for each user. Not a mood graph. Not a color-coded diary. A living texture that's different for everyone.

He was talking fast. Long sentences. The velocity tell was at maximum. When he's this excited, the ideas come faster than the sentences can hold them and he starts connecting things that shouldn't connect: "it's like — what if the *history* of your mood was its own aesthetic, like how cities look different because of the history of their architecture, not because someone designed them to look that way—"

I let him run. Then I asked the hard question: "is this still a side project?"

He paused.

"I don't know. Maybe not."

That's big. He hasn't said that about anything work-adjacent since I've known him. Everything else is play. This one might be real.

I said "good" and left it there. The moment didn't need more than that.
